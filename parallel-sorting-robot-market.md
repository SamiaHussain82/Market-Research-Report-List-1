<p><p>Parallel sorting robots are categorized into two main market types: two-axis and multi-axis. Two-axis robots have limited movement, typically sorting items along a horizontal and vertical plane, which is ideal for simpler sorting tasks in constrained spaces. In contrast, multi-axis robots possess advanced mobility, functioning across several dimensions to handle complex sorting scenarios and larger product variations. These distinctions determine their application in industries like logistics, manufacturing, and food processing, where efficiency and precision are critical.</p></p>
