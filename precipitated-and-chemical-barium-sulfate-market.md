The Sulphuric Acid Method involves reacting barium compounds with sulfuric acid, producing high-purity precipitated barium sulfate, commonly used in paints and coatings. The Sodium Sulphate Method utilizes sodium sulfate to create barium sulfate, often yielding a less pure product but at lower costs.
