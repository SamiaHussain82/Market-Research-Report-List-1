<p><p>The Automotive Anti-Lock Braking System (ABS) enhances vehicle safety by preventing wheel lock-up during hard braking, which helps maintain steering control. The market comprises two main types: Pneumatic ABS, which uses air pressure to modulate brake force, typically found in heavy vehicles, and Hydraulic ABS, employing hydraulic fluid to achieve similar results, predominantly used in passenger cars. Both systems aim to reduce stopping distances and improve vehicle stability on slippery surfaces, catering to diverse automotive needs.</p></p>
