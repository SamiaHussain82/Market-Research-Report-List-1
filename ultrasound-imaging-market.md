<p><p>The ultrasound imaging market consists of various types, each serving specific diagnostic purposes. </p><p>- 2D imaging provides flat, cross-sectional views of organs, aiding in basic assessments. </p><p>- 3D imaging offers volumetric representations, enhancing anatomical visualization. </p><p>- 4D imaging adds the dimension of time to 3D images, enabling real-time observation of moving structures. </p><p>- Doppler imaging measures blood flow and can detect abnormalities in vascular health. </p><p>- Other specialized technologies may include elastography and contrast-enhanced ultrasound, expanding diagnostic capabilities further.</p></p>
