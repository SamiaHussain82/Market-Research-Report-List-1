<p><ul><li>Wet Friction Clutch</li><li>Dry Friction Clutch</li><li>Electromagnetic Clutch</li><li>Others</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The light vehicle clutch market comprises several types, each serving distinct functions. Wet friction clutches use lubricating fluids to manage heat and provide smoother engagement, ideal for heavy-duty applications. Dry friction clutches operate without fluid, offering simpler designs and lighter weight, commonly found in standard vehicles. Electromagnetic clutches leverage electrical signals to engage or disengage, providing precise control and efficiency. Other types include continuously variable transmissions and multi-plate clutches, catering to diverse automotive needs and performance specifications.</p></p>
