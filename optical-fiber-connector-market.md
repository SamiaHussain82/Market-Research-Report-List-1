<p><p>The optical fiber connector market includes several types, each designed for specific applications. The FC connector features a screw-on mechanism for stability, making it suitable for high-vibration environments. SC connectors are popular for their easy push-pull design and low insertion loss, often used in single-mode applications. LC connectors are compact and ideal for high-density applications, while ST connectors use a bayonet mount for secure connections, commonly found in local area networks. Other connectors include MTP/MPO and E2000, which cater to specialized needs.</p></p>
