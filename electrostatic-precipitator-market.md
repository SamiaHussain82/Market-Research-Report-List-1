<p><p>The electrostatic precipitator (ESP) market is primarily divided into two types: wet and dry. Wet type ESPs use water to collect and remove particulate matter from flue gases, effectively capturing pollutants while minimizing dust re-entrainment. Dry type ESPs, on the other hand, utilize electric fields to separate particles without the use of water, making them suitable for industries with lower moisture content emissions. Both types are crucial for air pollution control, offering distinct advantages based on operational needs and environmental regulations.</p></p>
