<p><p>The aquatic animal vaccines market is primarily divided into two types: injectable vaccines and dip vaccines. Injectable vaccines are delivered directly into the fish or shrimp, providing targeted immunity and protection against specific diseases. They are often used for high-value species and in controlled environments. In contrast, dip vaccines involve immersing aquatic animals in a solution containing the vaccine, offering ease of administration and broader coverage in larger groups. Both types aim to improve health, reduce mortality, and enhance aquaculture productivity.</p></p>
