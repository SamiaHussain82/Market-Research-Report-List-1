<p><p>Low-light imaging technology primarily encompasses two types: Charge Coupled Devices (CCD) and Complementary Metal-Oxide-Semiconductor (CMOS) transistors. CCDs are known for their high sensitivity and image quality, making them suitable for applications like astronomy and medical imaging. Conversely, CMOS sensors have gained popularity for their lower power consumption, faster processing speeds, and integration capabilities, enabling their use in consumer electronics, smartphones, and surveillance systems. Both technologies are crucial in enhancing visibility in challenging lighting conditions.</p></p>
